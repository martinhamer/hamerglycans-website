About the Researcher

A retired nurse who solved his own case

Martin Hamer is a retired registered nurse with 45 years of clinical experience at institutions including the Texas Heart Institute and Vancouver General Hospital. He is not a dermatologist, gastroenterologist, or immunologist — which may be exactly why he noticed something the specialists missed.

In 2003, while following a modified Atkins diet, Hamer noticed his chronic skin condition had resolved. Systematic provocation-elimination testing confirmed the pattern: bovine dairy products and red meat reliably reactivated symptoms within two to four hours, while elimination produced sustained, reproducible clearance.

That observation launched a 23-year research programme into sialic acid biology, dietary xenoantigens, and the mechanism he now calls the Sialic-Glycan Inflammatory Cascade (SGIC) — the Hamer Loop.

"The hypothesis is simple. The immune implications are not." — Martin Hamer, Hamer Glycan Systems
